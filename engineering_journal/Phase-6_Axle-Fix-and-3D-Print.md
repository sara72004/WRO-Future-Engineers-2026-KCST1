# Phase 6: Axle Fix and 3D Printed Solution

## Problem Identified
During testing, the front wheel axle connected to the steering servo was not fitting tightly inside the wheels.

This caused:
- unstable steering behavior
- shaking and vibration in the front wheels
- inconsistent turning accuracy

The looseness between the axle and the wheel reduced the overall control of the robot, especially during turns.

---

## Attempted Solutions
Initially, we considered:
- applying glue to secure the axle
- adjusting the existing axle manually

However, these solutions were not reliable or precise enough.

---

## Final Solution: 3D Printed Axle

We designed and produced a custom 3D printed axle that fits perfectly inside the wheel.

### Improvements achieved:
- tight and stable connection between axle and wheel
- elimination of unwanted movement
- improved steering precision
- more consistent robot behavior during turns

---

## Implementation
The new axle was installed in the front wheel steering system.

The design ensured:
- correct alignment with the servo
- proper fit inside the wheel hub
- durability during movement

---

## Result
After installing the 3D printed axle:
- the steering became more stable
- wheel shaking was eliminated
- turning accuracy improved significantly

This modification directly improved the robot's performance in navigation tasks.

---

## Conclusion
This improvement shows the importance of mechanical precision in robot design.

By identifying the issue and developing a custom 3D printed solution, we were able to significantly enhance the robot’s reliability and control.
